Autonomous Navigation for Smart Cities

Authors: P. Reddy, M. Khan

Year: 2024

This cutting-edge publication presents an in-depth exploration into the development and integration of autonomous navigation technologies in urban environments. Developed by scholars at Malla Reddy University, it addresses the growing need for intelligent transportation systems to meet the demands of modern smart cities.

About the Book

"Autonomous Navigation for Smart Cities" delves into the fundamental and advanced technologies that enable vehicles and systems to navigate complex urban landscapes without human intervention. The book combines theoretical foundations with real-world applications, focusing on AI algorithms, sensor fusion, and smart infrastructure. It is an essential guide for researchers, city planners, and engineers working in the domain of smart mobility and autonomous systems.

Key Highlights

  • Introduction to autonomous vehicle systems and smart city frameworks
  • Role of AI, computer vision, and machine learning in navigation
  • Sensor technologies including LiDAR, radar, GPS, and IMU
  • Vehicle-to-Everything (V2X) communication protocols
  • Urban mobility challenges and proposed solutions
  • Simulation tools and datasets for smart navigation testing

Applications in Smart Cities

The implementation of autonomous navigation systems in smart cities is set to revolutionize urban living by offering:

  • Traffic optimization and congestion reduction
  • Real-time route planning and dynamic traffic control
  • Improved public transport through autonomous shuttles and buses
  • Enhanced safety and reduced human error in driving
  • Integration with IoT-enabled city infrastructure
  • Energy-efficient and sustainable urban mobility solutions

Future Prospects

As cities evolve, autonomous navigation will become a critical component of urban planning. This publication outlines upcoming trends in AI policy, ethical considerations in autonomous decision-making, and collaborative infrastructure development between governments and tech industries. It serves as a roadmap for building smarter, safer, and more efficient cities.
